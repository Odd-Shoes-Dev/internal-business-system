## 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 1.1 "Confidential Information" means any information disclosed by Disclosing Party to Receiving Party, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by observation, including but not limited to:

**Technical Information:**
- Software source code, algorithms, and architecture
- System designs and specifications
- Technical documentation and API details
- Database schemas and structures
- Security protocols and measures
- Integration methodologies
- Performance metrics and benchmarks

**Business Information:**
- Customer lists and data
- Pricing strategies and models
- Financial information and projections
- Marketing plans and strategies
- Sales data and forecasts
- Business plans and strategies
- Product roadmaps and features
- Vendor and supplier information

**Proprietary Information:**
- Trade secrets
- Intellectual property
- Research and development
- Inventions and innovations
- Know-how and processes

### 1.2 Information designated as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or bearing a similar legend.

### 1.3 Information that, due to its nature or the circumstances of disclosure, a reasonable person would understand to be confidential.

---

## 2. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information does not include information that:

**a) Public Domain:**  
Was publicly available at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by Receiving Party.

**b) Prior Knowledge:**  
Was rightfully in Receiving Party's possession prior to disclosure by Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records.

**c) Third-Party Disclosure:**  
Is rightfully received by Receiving Party from a third party without confidentiality restrictions and without breach of any obligation to Disclosing Party.

**d) Independent Development:**  
Is independently developed by Receiving Party without reference to or use of Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records.

**e) Authorized Disclosure:**  
Is disclosed with prior written approval of Disclosing Party.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

### 3.1 Non-Disclosure
Receiving Party shall:
- Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence
- Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party
- Use the same degree of care to protect Confidential Information as used for its own confidential information (but not less than reasonable care)
- Not use Confidential Information for any purpose other than the Purpose

### 3.2 Limited Disclosure
Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to:
- Its employees, officers, directors, and agents ("Representatives") who:
  - Have a legitimate need to know for the Purpose
  - Have been informed of the confidential nature
  - Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as this Agreement

### 3.3 Responsibility
Receiving Party is responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives and shall take reasonable measures to restrain Representatives from unauthorized disclosure or use.

### 3.4 Security Measures
Receiving Party shall implement appropriate security measures to protect Confidential Information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

---

## 4. REQUIRED DISCLOSURE

### 4.1 Legal Compulsion
If Receiving Party is required by law, court order, or government regulation to disclose Confidential Information, Receiving Party shall:

a) Immediately notify Disclosing Party in writing (if legally permitted)  
b) Cooperate with Disclosing Party's efforts to obtain protective order or other relief  
c) Disclose only that portion of Confidential Information legally required  
d) Use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment of disclosed information

### 4.2 No Notice
If Receiving Party is legally prohibited from providing notice, it shall still comply with subsections (c) and (d) above.

---

## 5. OWNERSHIP AND RETURN OF MATERIALS

### 5.1 Ownership
All Confidential Information remains the sole property of Disclosing Party. No license or right is granted by disclosure except as expressly stated in this Agreement.

### 5.2 Return or Destruction
Upon Disclosing Party's written request or termination of this Agreement, Receiving Party shall:
- Promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information in any form
- Certify in writing such return or destruction within 10 days
- Delete electronic copies and clear them from system backups (within reasonable timeframes for backup systems)

### 5.3 Retention Exception
Receiving Party may retain one copy of Confidential Information solely for legal compliance purposes, subject to continued confidentiality obligations.

### 6.2 Disclaimer

## 8. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 8.1 Term
This Agreement is effective as of the Effective Date and continues for:
- **Two (2) years** from the Effective Date, OR
- Until terminated by either party with thirty (30) days written notice

### 8.2 Survival
Confidentiality obligations survive termination for:
- **Five (5) years** for general Confidential Information
- **Indefinitely** for trade secrets as defined by applicable law

### 8.3 Effect of Termination
Termination does not relieve Receiving Party of obligations accrued prior to termination.
